project summary
During my third year of Electrical Engineering, I did a minor in entrepreneurship. During this time, I interviewed an elder care worker to understand how to relieve their work stress.
This revealed that they spend a lot of time manually monitoring medicine storage temperatures. To relieve this burden, my friend and I designed a thermostat with automated monitoring and control capabilities.
This page captures the sensing strategy, control loop design, and risk-mitigation approach.
feature set
• Automated medicine storage temperature monitoring with dual-sensor redundancy
• Safety alerts and cutoff logic for out-of-range conditions
• Continuous logging, diagnostics, and status reporting for care staff
project status
Current focus: validating the temperature monitoring workflow and ensuring the firmware reliably detects and reports unsafe storage conditions.
